Dai-san Daiba/Dai-roku Daiba


.::Left: A suijo-bus passing in front of Dai-roku Daiba, Vamdemon's base::.

The name "Odaiba" comes from a series of six island fortresses constructed in 1853 by the Tokugawa shogunate in order to protect Tokyo from attack by sea. Daiba refers to the cannon batteries placed on the islands. In 1928, the Dai-san Daiba ("No. 3 Battery") was refurbished and opened to the public as Daibakōen ("Daiba Park").


.::Daiba Anchorage viewed from northeast corner of the park::.

From the originally planned 11 batteries, only five were ever finished. Prior to the mid-1960s, all except two batteries were either removed for unhindered passage of ships or incorporated into the Shinagawa port facilities and Tennozu island. In 1979 the then-called landfill no. 13 (now Minato-ku Daiba, Shinagawa-ku Higashi-Yashio and Koto-ku Aomi districts), was made to connect with the old Dai-san Daiba. Dai-roku Daiba ("No. 6 Battery") was left to nature. Both are designated as heritage sites.


.::The inner area of the battery::.

The Dai-roku Daiba is featured in Digimon Adventure as Vamdemon's base, and the Dai-san Daiba/Daibakōen in Digimon Adventure 02 as the meeting place in episode 17, Odaiba Memorial. Later in the series, the sunken area of the battery is used to conceal Imperialdramon as the Chosen Children embark on their journey around the world.
