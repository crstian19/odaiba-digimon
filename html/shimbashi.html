Shimbashi

JR Shimbashi Station is located in a district of the same name on the mainland side of Tōkyō Bay. It was the Tōkyō terminus of the first railway in Japan in 1872, the Tōkaidō Main Line, and was one of the first railway stations built in Japan. Shimbashi remains a major railway hub to this day.

The present-day structure opened in 1909 as Karasumori Station on the Yamanote Line. With the extension of the Tōkaidō Main Line along its modern-day route to the new terminus at Tōkyō Station in 1914, the original station was demolished. Japan's first subway line, operated by the Tōkyō Underground Railroad Company, was extended to Shimbashi in 1934. In January 1939, the Tōkyō Rapid Railway Company began to stop at Shimbashi on its line from Shibuya. The two would later merge to form the Tōkyō Metro Ginza Line. The Toei Asakusa Line began service to Shimbashi in 1968, and the elevated Yurikamome station opened in an adjacent building in 1995. It is the Tōkyō-side terminum for the line. In 2003, a reconstruction of the original Shimbashi Station building and part of the platforms was completed, which currently houses a railway history exhibit and a restaurant.
