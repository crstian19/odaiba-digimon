Tokyo Tower
The icon of Tokyo Bay

Tokyo Tower is a communications tower located in Shibakoen, Minato-ku. The 332.6-meter-tall orange and white lattice tower is the tallest self-supporting steel structure in the world, the tallest artificial structure in Japan and the 11th tallest tower in the world as of 2008. Built in 1958 to resemble the Eiffel Tower, it supports an antenna that broadcasts television and radio signals for media outlets such as NHK, TBS and Fuji TV.

In addition to being a television and radio communications tower, Tokyo Tower functions as a major Tokyo tourist site. Over 2.5 million people annually visit the tower's recreational Foot Town and two observations decks. Foot Town is a 4-story building stationed directly under the tower which houses several museums, restaurants and shops. Departing from here, guests can also visit the two observation decks. The 2-story Main Observatory is located at 150 m, while the smaller Special Observatory is at 250 m. The tower is constructed of steel, a third of which was from recycled World War II scrap steel. The observation decks offer an excellent view of the Rainbow Bridge, Fuji TV and the surrounding landscape (Daibakōen, Odaiba-kaihinkōen, Aqua City, DECKS Tokyo Beach), while on the opposite side of the bay the Fuji TV observation deck is an excellent vantage point to see Tokyo Tower from. Since it is mainly surrounded by low-rise buildings, Tokyo Tower can be seen from many other points in the central wards of Tokyo, such as Roppongi Hills, Odaiba/Tokyo Bay, the east gardens of the Imperial Palace, and the southern promenade of Shinjuku Station.

From dusk to midnight, 176 floodlights completely illuminate the tower. Sodium vapor lamps are used from October 2 to July 6 to cover the tower in an orange color. From July 7 to October 1, the lights are changed to metal halide lamps to illuminate the tower with a white color. The reasoning behind the change is seasonal: it is believed that orange is a warmer color and helps to offset the cold winter months. Conversely, white is seen as a cool color and helps during the hot summer months. The Millennium Lighting was designed by Motoko Ishii and is occasionally changed to specific, unique arrangement for special events, such as blue, white and red for Christmas in past years, pink for National Breast Cancer Awareness Month, green for World Diabetes Day, and others. The seasonal lightings change by year and event and are often not re-used. Unfortunately, the planned opening of the taller Shin Tokyo Tower in 2011 is expected to further depress Tokyo Tower's profits as broadcasters move to the new tower, so the future of the iconic tower is as of yet unknown.

Just as the Eiffel Tower is often used in popular culture to immediately locate a scene in Paris, France, Tokyo Tower is often used in the same way to locate a scene in Tokyo. Sailor Moon, Tenchi Muyo!, Detective Conan, X, Card Captor Sakura and Magic Knight Rayearth are just a few examples of shows that feature it. CLAMP is famous for utilizing the Tower in their works. The Tower is also frequently seen in the Japanese kaiju film genre, as the location of climatic battles between Godzilla, Mothra, and King Kong, as well as in more recent drama films like the aptly-titled Tokyo Tower and Always: San-chōme no Yūhi.

DeathMeramon" when Sora and Mimi use the observation deck to search for the Eighth Child, but also for its air-conditioner. It is severely damaged in a fight between Birdramon, DeathMeramon, Kabuterimon and Greymon, and the area above the Main Observatory is almost severed entirely.
